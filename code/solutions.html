Effects of Climate Change in Ghana

How climate change is impacting different sectors and regions in Ghana

Impact on Agriculture

Climate change is severely affecting Ghana's agricultural sector, which employs about 60% of the workforce:

  • Reduced crop yields due to changing rainfall patterns and prolonged droughts
  • Shifting growing seasons making traditional farming calendars unreliable
  • Increased pest and disease outbreaks affecting crops like cocoa, Ghana's main export
  • Declining soil fertility due to erosion from intense rainfall events

Northern Ghana is particularly vulnerable, with some areas experiencing yield reductions of up to 40% for staple crops like maize and millet.

Water Resources Stress

Climate change is altering Ghana's water resources:

  • Reduced water levels in major rivers like the Volta, affecting hydropower generation
  • Increased frequency of both floods and droughts
  • Declining groundwater recharge in northern regions
  • Water quality issues due to increased runoff during heavy rains

The Akosombo Dam, which provides most of Ghana's electricity, has experienced reduced capacity during droughts, leading to power rationing.

Declining water levels in Lake Volta during dry seasons

Coastal Erosion and Flooding

Ghana's coastline is particularly vulnerable to climate change:

  • Sea level rise of 3.4mm per year is causing coastal erosion
  • Increased frequency of storm surges flooding coastal communities
  • Saltwater intrusion contaminating freshwater sources
  • Threats to infrastructure like the Takoradi and Tema ports

Some coastal communities have already been displaced, with projections suggesting up to 30% of Ghana's coastline could be lost by 2050.

Human Health Impacts

Climate change is affecting public health in Ghana:

  • Increased incidence of malaria as mosquitoes expand their range
  • Heat-related illnesses becoming more common
  • Water-borne diseases like cholera during floods
  • Malnutrition due to reduced food security
  • Respiratory diseases from dust during dry seasons

The Ghana Health Service reports climate change could reverse progress made in controlling infectious diseases.

Biodiversity Loss

Ghana's rich biodiversity is under threat from climate change:

  • Shifting ecosystems affecting species distribution
  • Coral bleaching along coastal areas
  • Declining populations of native species unable to adapt
  • Increased invasive species taking advantage of changing conditions

Protected areas like Mole National Park are seeing changes in animal behavior and vegetation patterns.
