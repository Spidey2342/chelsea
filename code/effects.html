Causes of Climate Change in Ghana

Understanding the drivers of climate change in the Ghanaian context

Deforestation

Ghana has one of the highest deforestation rates in Africa (2% annually), primarily due to agricultural expansion, illegal logging, and fuelwood collection. Forests that act as carbon sinks are being destroyed.

Bush Burning

Traditional farming practices involve slash-and-burn agriculture, releasing significant amounts of carbon dioxide and other greenhouse gases into the atmosphere.

Transportation Emissions

Increasing vehicle ownership, often with older, less efficient models, contributes to air pollution and greenhouse gas emissions, particularly in urban areas like Accra and Kumasi.

Industrial Activities

Growing industrial sector, including mining and manufacturing, often relies on fossil fuels and emits greenhouse gases. The oil and gas industry is a particular concern.

Energy Production

Despite progress in renewable energy, Ghana still depends heavily on fossil fuels for electricity generation, especially during droughts when hydroelectric capacity is reduced.

Waste Management

Poor waste management leads to methane emissions from landfills. Open burning of waste is common in many communities, releasing harmful gases.
