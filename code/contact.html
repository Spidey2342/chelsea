Climate Change Solutions for Ghana

Actions being taken and what more can be done to address climate change

National Climate Policies

  • National Climate Change Policy (2013): Provides framework for climate action across sectors
  • Ghana's NDCs: Commits to reducing emissions by 15-45% by 2030 with international support
  • Renewable Energy Master Plan: Aims for 10% renewable energy by 2030
  • National Adaptation Strategy: Focuses on building resilience in vulnerable sectors

Key Programs

  • Planting for Food and Jobs (climate-smart agriculture)
  • Ghana Landscape Restoration and Small-Scale Mining Project
  • National REDD+ Strategy to reduce deforestation

Local Level Solutions

Climate-Smart Agriculture

Adoption of drought-resistant crop varieties, conservation agriculture, and agroforestry

Ecosystem Restoration

Mangrove restoration along coasts to protect against erosion and storms

Water Conservation

Rainwater harvesting systems and small-scale irrigation projects

30% potential utilized

Agricultural waste can be converted to energy. Several waste-to-energy plants are planned.

Take Action Against Climate Change

Individuals

  • Reduce energy consumption at home
  • Use public transport or carpool
  • Practice proper waste management
  • Plant trees in your community
  • Educate others about climate change

Schools

  • Establish environmental clubs
  • Implement recycling programs
  • Create school gardens
  • Organize climate awareness campaigns
  • Adopt energy-saving measures

Businesses

  • Adopt sustainable practices
  • Invest in renewable energy
  • Reduce, reuse, recycle materials
  • Support climate-friendly policies
  • Engage in corporate social responsibility
