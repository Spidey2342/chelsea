About Climate Change in Ghana

Understanding the context and importance of climate action in Ghana

Ghana's Climate Vulnerability

Ghana, like many African nations, is particularly vulnerable to the impacts of climate change despite contributing less than 1% to global greenhouse gas emissions. The country's economy relies heavily on climate-sensitive sectors such as agriculture, forestry, and fisheries, which employ about 60% of the population.

Historical Climate Trends

Over the past 50 years, Ghana has experienced:

  • A gradual increase in average temperature by about 1Â°C
  • Declining rainfall in most ecological zones, particularly in the north
  • Increased frequency and intensity of extreme weather events
  • Rising sea levels along the coast (3.4mm per year)

National Response

Ghana has shown commitment to addressing climate change through various policies and programs:

  • National Climate Change Policy (2013)
  • Ghana's Nationally Determined Contributions (NDCs) under the Paris Agreement
  • Establishment of the Ghana Climate Innovation Center
  • Integration of climate change into national development planning
